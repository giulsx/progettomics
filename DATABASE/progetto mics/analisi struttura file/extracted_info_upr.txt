- Activity Name: anaerobic digestion of manure
- Included Activities Start: Input of livestock manure (cattle slurry, pig slurry, cattle manure) to incoming storage at the biogas plant.
- Included Activities End: The treatment includes storage of the substrates at the biogas fermentation plant, anerobic fermentation, as well as the storage of digestate after fermentation. The activity ends with the biogas and digestate being available at the biogas plant. Furthermore, the calorific value of the biogas only accounts for the methane content excluding the H2S presence in it. Glycerine was removed as input in the anaerobic digestion (AD) process. Water content of digestate is 95% in wet weight basis. Finally, the dataset incorporates emissions of CO2, CH4, NH3 and N2O to air due to the storage of the substrates before the AD process as well as from storage of the digestate after the AD process. The storage of the substrate before and after the AD process is assumed to be an under cover system.

General Comments:
- Inventory refers to 1m3 of biogas (which corresponds to 33 kg of farmyard manure). Electricity consumption and emissions represent the average biogas production in a digestion plant based on 34 biogas plants in Switzerland.  Infrastructure expenditures are included.
